Organisms Most Affected

Learn which marine life is especially vulnerable to ocean acidification

Oysters & Shellfish

Shellfish that build calcium carbonate shells — such as oysters, clams, and scallops — rely on carbonate ions that become less available in acidified waters. This can slow growth or reduce survival. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

Pteropods (Sea Butterflies)

These tiny planktonic snails have thin aragonite shells that are especially vulnerable to dissolution in lower pH water. They are key food for many fish species, so impacts can ripple through food webs. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

Coral Reefs

Corals build massive calcium carbonate structures that form reef habitat. Acidification reduces their ability to grow and maintain skeletons, weakening reefs that support diverse marine life. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

Coccolithophores

These tiny calcifying phytoplankton help form marine carbon cycles. Acidification can reduce their calcification, altering biogeochemical processes and food availability for higher trophic levels. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

Sea Urchins & Echinoderms

Sea urchins and similar species rely on calcified structures. Acidic conditions can slow skeletal formation and reduce larval survival, affecting their populations. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}
