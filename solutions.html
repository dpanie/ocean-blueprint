Solutions

From restoring local seagrass to global carbon policy, we have the tools to protect our blue planet.

Natural & Local Solutions

Restoring ecosystems that naturally balance ocean chemistry by absorbing $CO_2$.

Restoration

Eelgrass Meadows

Seagrasses act as "carbon sinks," pulling $CO_2$ out of the water through photosynthesis. This creates a "halo effect" of safer, less acidic water for nearby shellfish.

Learn More
Adaptation

Hatchery Treatment

Oyster hatcheries can now monitor water chemistry in real-time. By adding alkaline buffers—essentially "antacids"—they can protect vulnerable larvae from corrosive water.

The Oyster Story
Innovation

Kelp Co-farming

Seaweed grows incredibly fast and sucks up huge amounts of $CO_2$. Farming kelp alongside mussels and oysters provides a natural chemical shield for the shells.

The "Big Bets"

Emerging technologies designed to scale up carbon removal across the open ocean.

Tech

Electrochemical Removal

New technologies are being tested that use electricity to pull $CO_2$ directly out of seawater, allowing the ocean to absorb more from the atmosphere without becoming more acidic.

Explore Tech
Geochemistry

Mineral Enhancement

By adding crushed alkaline minerals (like olivine) to the ocean, scientists hope to replicate the earth's natural weathering process to neutralize acidity at scale.
