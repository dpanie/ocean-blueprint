Ocean chemistry

What is Ocean Acidification?

Ocean acidification is a shift in seawater chemistry driven by extra atmospheric CO₂. When CO₂ dissolves in seawater, it increases hydrogen ions (H⁺), which lowers pH and converts carbonate (CO₃²⁻) into bicarbonate (HCO₃⁻).

pH measures H⁺ (log scale)
Carbonate system: CO₂ / H₂CO₃ / HCO₃⁻ / CO₃²⁻
Acidification ≠ “becoming acid”

1) CO₂ enters seawater

CO₂ moves across the air–sea boundary. Waves and wind speed up this exchange.

2) H⁺ increases → pH drops

Dissolved CO₂ forms carbonic acid that partially dissociates, raising H⁺. pH goes down.

3) Carbonate shifts to bicarbonate

Extra H⁺ pushes the carbonate system away from CO₃²⁻ and toward HCO₃⁻.

Definition (no fluff)

Ocean acidification = an increase in seawater H⁺ (hydrogen ions) caused by uptake of atmospheric CO₂, which lowers pH and changes how inorganic carbon is distributed among CO₂(aq), H₂CO₃, HCO₃⁻, and CO₃²⁻.

Stop misusing “pH”

pH is a log scale: a small pH drop is a big change in H⁺. So “slightly lower pH” is not a casual statement—it means meaningfully higher H⁺.

  • pH = −log₁₀[H⁺]
  • Lower pH ⟹ higher [H⁺]
  • “Acidification” can happen even if seawater stays above pH 7 (still basic).
Core idea: This is not a vibe or a headline. It’s a predictable equilibrium shift in the carbonate system.

The key reactions

Read this like a chain: CO₂ enters → acid forms → H⁺ rises → the balance shifts. You’re not “memorizing.” You’re tracking direction.

CO₂ + H₂O ⇌ H₂CO₃ CO₂ hydrates to carbonic acid (often grouped with CO₂(aq))
H₂CO₃ ⇌ H⁺ + HCO₃⁻ acid dissociation produces H⁺ and bicarbonate
HCO₃⁻ ⇌ H⁺ + CO₃²⁻ more H⁺ drives equilibrium away from carbonate

What “carbonate system” really means

Seawater holds inorganic carbon mostly as bicarbonate, with smaller fractions as dissolved CO₂/carbonic acid and carbonate. Adding CO₂ doesn’t just lower pH—it re-partitions the system, increasing HCO₃⁻ and decreasing CO₃²⁻.

  • DIC (dissolved inorganic carbon) = CO₂(aq) + H₂CO₃ + HCO₃⁻ + CO₃²⁻
  • OA changes the fractions of these species at equilibrium.
  • That chemistry shift is the definition—impacts are downstream.

What it is not

These mistakes instantly signal weak understanding. Don’t make them on your site.

Not “the ocean turning into acid”

Acidification means moving toward lower pH, not necessarily below pH 7.

Not only about pH

pH is one lens. The deeper story is the carbonate-system shift.

Not a one-step reaction

It’s a set of equilibria. That’s why conditions (mixing, temperature) matter.
